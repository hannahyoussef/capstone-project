# MatchPoint ⭐ Top Choice

I've been playing tennis recreationally for years, but one thing I've noticed is that most amateur players don't have a good way to track their improvement. We take lessons, play matches, and practice regularly, but the details usually end up scattered across notes, text messages, or not recorded at all.

MatchPoint would be a tennis development platform designed specifically for recreational players. Users could log matches, record practice sessions, save lesson notes, set goals, and track their progress over time.

The idea behind MatchPoint is simple: improvement is difficult to measure if you don't have a record of where you've been. By combining match history, training data, and performance analytics in one place, players can gain deeper insight into their development and make more informed decisions about their training.

## Potential Data Sources

- ATP/WTA player and ranking data
- Tennis statistics APIs
- User-generated match and practice data
